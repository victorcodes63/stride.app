1. Product at a Glance

What is Backbone HR?

Backbone HR is an all-in-one Human Resource Information System (HRIS) built for Kenyan and East African businesses. It covers the full employee lifecycle:

Hire → Onboard → Time → Pay → Perform → Exit (and Bill, for HR outsourcers)

Unlike multi-tenant SaaS platforms, each client gets a dedicated deployment: their own database, domain, branding, and environment. Data is isolated. The client owns their data.

One-Line Pitch

Backbone HR gives mid-market East African employers a right-sized HR and payroll operating system — transparent in cost, built for local statutory compliance, and directly accountable to the people building it.

Qualification Signals (Good Fit)

  • Payroll or HR data scattered across spreadsheets, email, and WhatsApp
  • Multi-location or multi-country workforce (Kenya/Uganda especially)
  • Field or shift-based staff who need mobile self-service
  • Finance wants cost visibility by department, branch, station, or cost centre
  • Frustrated with opaque enterprise pricing or slow vendor support
  • HR outsourcer billing multiple end-clients from one workforce

Disqualification Signals (Proceed Carefully)

  • Needs pan-African statutory engines for 5+ countries on day one
  • Requires SAP/Oracle-level ERP integration before go-live (possible later, not v1)
  • Wants only a lightweight leave tracker with no payroll
  • Expects native iOS/Android apps immediately (we are PWA-first)
  • Needs performance management as a core requirement today (roadmap item)

3. Value Proposition by Stakeholder

For Finance

  • Predictable total cost — transparent monthly fee, modular scope, no surprise module charges
  • Payroll control layer — approvals, audit trail, statutory logic built in
  • Cost visibility — headcount and payroll by entity, department, branch, cost centre
  • Compliance confidence — Kenya PAYE, NSSF, SHIF, Affordable Housing Levy; Uganda support
  • Lower manual effort — fewer spreadsheet reconciliations and correction cycles

For HR

  • Single system of record — employee master data, documents, lifecycle events
  • Less repetitive work — ESS handles payslip and leave requests
  • Structured workflows — onboarding, offboarding, disciplinary, grievance
  • Recruitment pipeline — jobs, applications, interviews, hire conversion
  • Credential tracking — licence and certification expiry alerts

For Employees & Managers

  • Mobile-first ESS (PWA) — payslips, leave, attendance, documents on phone
  • Simple approvals — managers approve leave from their device
  • Transparency — employees see balances, payslips, and policies without calling HR

For the Business (Executive)

  • Adoption-friendly — designed for field and station staff, not only HQ
  • Direct accountability — access to the builder, not a support queue
  • Flexible growth path — start with payroll control, extend to attendance, ATS, HSE, analytics
  • Dedicated deployment — data isolation, white-label branding, client owns their data

How to talk about price:

You are not buying software for KES 150,000. You are buying payroll control, compliance visibility, staff self-service, and management reporting for roughly KES 300 per employee per month. That is a small cost relative to payroll value, statutory exposure, and manual effort saved.

Important: Always confirm scope in writing. Price varies by headcount, modules, entities, and integration requirements.

7. Discovery Questions

Use these to uncover pain and qualify the opportunity.

Opening (any buyer)

  1. How do you manage employee records and payroll today?
  2. How many employees, and across how many locations or countries?
  3. What breaks most often — payroll accuracy, leave tracking, approvals, or reporting?
  4. Who is involved in a typical payroll run, and how long does it take?

Finance-specific

  1. What does payroll look like across entities — separate runs, consolidated review, or both?
  2. Where do payroll delays come from: HR inputs, approvals, attendance/overtime, statutory checks, or reporting?
  3. How do you currently see staff cost by department, branch, station, or entity?
  4. What report do you wish you could get instantly but currently wait for?
  5. What would make this decision easy to defend internally?

HR-specific

  1. How much time does your team spend on payslip requests and leave enquiries?
  2. How do you track onboarding, contracts, and certification renewals today?
  3. Do you recruit internally or use external tools for hiring?

Operations-specific

  1. Do you have shift workers, station staff, or depot teams?
  2. How is attendance captured today — manual, biometric, or not at all?
  3. Does overtime or attendance feed into payroll automatically?

The best closing discovery question

What would you need to see in the first 90 days to feel confident this was the right decision?

9. Objection Handling

Objection Response
"Why are you cheaper than enterprise vendors?" We price for your actual footprint, not large-vendor overhead. The advantage is predictable total cost — not just lower price.
"Are you established enough?" Fair concern. We reduce risk with a written SLA, controlled pilot, clear success criteria, data ownership terms, and a clean exit clause.
"What if payroll fails?" Parallel validation before go-live. We compare outputs against your current payroll, confirm statutory calculations, and only go live after finance sign-off.
"What happens if something breaks?" Critical payroll-period issues get priority handling with direct escalation. SLA defines response times in writing.
"Can this integrate with our other systems?" Yes — APIs and structured exports. Start with payroll reports and journal exports; extend to biometrics, ERP, Power BI as needed.
"Why not choose the bigger vendor?" Valid option. The question is fit. We offer Kenya/Uganda depth, transparent cost, faster adaptation, and lower adoption friction — with pilot-based risk control.
"Will this scale as we grow?" Yes. Start with HR and payroll control; extend to attendance, HSE, ERP, and analytics on the same foundation.
"Is performance management included?" Not yet live. Core HR, payroll, leave, time, ATS, and ESS are market-ready. Performance is on the roadmap — we will not oversell it.

11. Implementation Overview

What the client gets:

  1. Dedicated instance — own database (Neon PostgreSQL), hosting (Vercel), custom domain
  2. Branded deployment — logo, colours, org name, white-label option
  3. Module configuration — only licensed modules enabled
  4. Data migration — employee and payroll data imported with parallel validation
  5. Go-live support — founder-led, with finance sign-off before live payroll

Typical timeline:

Phase Duration Activities
Scoping 1–2 weeks Modules, entities, integrations, success criteria
Provisioning < 1 day DB, hosting, branding, env configuration
Configuration 1–3 weeks Payroll rules, leave policies, org structure, users
Migration & validation 2–4 weeks Data import, parallel payroll runs, UAT
Go-live 1 payroll cycle Finance sign-off, ESS rollout to employees

Provisioning target: Under 4 hours for a fresh dedicated instance.
